What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on your weight in relation to your height. It provides a simple numeric value that classifies people as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters (kg/mÂ²). While BMI does not directly measure body fat, it is a useful screening tool for identifying potential health risks associated with weight.

Why is BMI Important?

Maintaining a healthy BMI is important for overall health and well-being. It is associated with a lower risk of developing chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, hypertension, and certain cancers.

BMI can also help healthcare professionals assess an individual's risk factors and provide personalized recommendations for improving health outcomes. It serves as a starting point for discussions about weight management and lifestyle changes.

Tips for Maintaining a Healthy Lifestyle

  • Follow a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats.
  • Engage in regular physical activity, aiming for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise most days of the week.
  • Get adequate sleep each night, aiming for 7-9 hours for adults.
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption to moderate levels.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Visit your healthcare provider regularly for check-ups and screenings.
